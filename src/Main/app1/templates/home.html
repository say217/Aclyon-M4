Capabilities

Everything Needed
for Modern Research

From ingestion to insight — Aclyon handles the full research pipeline so you can focus on discovery.

Semantic Retrieval

Search thousands of papers using hybrid vector and keyword search. BGE embeddings capture contextual meaning; BM25 ensures exact-match precision — both combined and reranked in real time.

AI Research Assistant

Ask natural language questions and receive grounded, evidence-backed answers. The Qwen reasoning engine synthesizes retrieved passages into coherent, citation-linked responses.

Paper Comparison

Structured side-by-side analysis of methodologies, datasets, limitations, and findings across multiple papers. Understand consensus, disagreements, and research gaps instantly.

Evidence-Based Responses

Every answer is anchored to retrieved research. Citations are surfaced inline, with passage-level provenance so you can verify claims and follow sources directly into the literature.

Process

From PDF to Research Insight

A multi-stage agent pipeline that ingests raw papers and surfaces precise, grounded intelligence.

01
Upload Papers
Drag in PDFs, arXiv links, or DOIs. Aclyon handles parsing, structure extraction, and metadata.
02
Index & Embed
Sections are chunked intelligently and embedded via BGE into a high-dimensional vector space.
03
Retrieve Evidence
Hybrid retrieval combines dense semantic vectors with sparse BM25 for full coverage.
04
Rerank Results
A cross-encoder reranker scores passages for relevance and filters noise before the LLM.
05
Generate Answer
Qwen reasons over the evidence and generates a grounded, citation-linked response.

Attention Is All You Need
Vaswani, A. · Shazeer, N. · Parmar, N. · et al. · NeurIPS 2017
Abstract
The dominant sequence transduction models are based on complex recurrent or convolutional neural networks. We propose a new simple network architecture, the Transformer, based solely on attention mechanisms, dispensing with recurrence and convolutions entirely. Experiments on two machine translation tasks show these models to be superior in quality...
The Transformer uses multi-head attention in three different ways. Queries come from the previous decoder layer, and the memory keys and values come from the output of the encoder.
We obtained 41.0 BLEU on the WMT 2014 English-to-French translation task, outperforming all previously published single models at less than 1/4 the training cost.
